Preparation

The National Disaster Coordinating Council (NDCC) has instructed all local government authorities in Luzon to evacuate citizens if necessary. They distributed humanitarian materials and other essentials in addition to the NDCC. President Gloria Macapagal Arroyo also asked PAGASA to monitor the super typhoon every two hours and update its website.[21] PAGASA also cautioned all places with public storm signals to be prepared for storm surges, landslides, and flash floods. The Coast Guard issued a red warning for the whole country, indicating that the agency will be monitoring the entire country, particularly Northern and Central Luzon. All ferry terminals on routes from Luzon to Visayas and vice versa were shut down, and fishermen have been urged not to go out.
